# VIRTUE SIGNAL: Mission -- "The Gym"

## The Pitch (one sentence)

Metal Gear Solid but the guards are women in lycra and the weapon that kills you is your own eyes.

---

## The Setup

The player needs something from a locker on the far side of a gym. A Network dead drop. The contact left a USB stick in locker 47. The gym is a Composter fitness studio in Shoreditch called "EMPOWER." It's women-dominated. The player (male) has a guest pass. Get in. Cross the gym floor. Reach the lockers. Get the USB. Get out.

Simple. Except every woman in the gym has UVA (Unwanted Visual Attention) detection. Look at any of them for more than 2 seconds and they report you. Three reports and the gym manager escorts you out. Mission failed.

---

## The Layout (one room, MGS-style top-down awareness)

The gym floor is a rectangular space. The player enters from the door at one end. The lockers are at the far end. Between them:

**Row 1: Treadmills.** Six women running, facing forward. Their vision cones point ahead, toward the mirrors on the front wall. Safe to pass behind them. NOT safe to pass in front (they can see you in the mirror). NOT safe to walk alongside (peripheral vision).

**Row 2: Free weights area.** Women doing exercises that involve looking in different directions. Squats (looking forward), deadlifts (looking down), overhead press (looking up), lateral raises (looking sideways). Their vision cones CHANGE with each rep. The player must time their movement to the exercise rhythm. She's looking up on the press, move now, she's looking down on the deadlift, you're clear, she's doing lateral raises and scanning the whole room, FREEZE.

**Row 3: Stretching zone.** The hardest section. Yoga mats. Women in positions that are extremely difficult not to glance at. The game is being honest about the difficulty: this is hard because human eyes are drawn to movement and bodies. That's not a moral failing. It's optics. The stretching zone is the game's version of an MGS laser grid. Get through it by looking at the floor, the ceiling, or your phone.

**Row 4: The corridor to the lockers.** Narrow. A personal trainer patrols it. She's the boss enemy. She walks slowly back and forth. Her vision cone is wide. She's LOOKING for men who are looking. If she spots you looking at ANYTHING other than straight ahead, instant report. No 2-second grace period. She's a Gaze Marshal in a crop top.

---

## The Mechanics

### The Gaze System

The player has a reticle showing where their character is looking. In normal gameplay, this is just the camera direction. In this mission, it becomes the threat.

**NPCs have gaze-detection zones.** If the player's reticle enters the zone around an NPC and stays for 2 seconds: report. The zone is roughly the NPC's body plus a small margin. Looking at the wall behind them: fine. Looking at the equipment: fine. Looking at THEM: report.

**The 2-second window is displayed as a small filling circle** around the reticle when it enters a detection zone. The player can see it filling. They have 2 seconds to look away. This creates constant micro-tension: the circle starts, you snap your gaze away, it resets. Start, snap, reset. Start, snap, reset. The rhythm of not looking.

### The Mirrors

The gym has mirrors on three walls. Mirrors REFLECT gaze detection. If the player looks at a mirror and a woman is visible in the reflection, the 2-second timer starts. The mirrors are the most dangerous element because they're everywhere and they multiply the threats. You look at a mirror to check if the path ahead is clear and accidentally make eye contact with someone's reflection. +3.

The mirrors also work in reverse: NPCs can spot you looking at them VIA mirrors. The treadmill women face the front mirror. If you're in front of them, they can see you in the mirror. Their gaze detection works through reflections.

### The Phone

The player can pull up their phone. While looking at the phone, the gaze reticle is locked to the phone screen. Safe from gaze detection. But: movement speed drops 50% and you can't see obstacles. You'll bump into a bench or trip over a dumbbell. Bumping into anything near a person: "unwanted physical proximity," +2.

The phone is the safe mode but it makes navigation harder. The player constantly toggles: phone up (safe, blind), phone down (fast, dangerous).

### The Exercises (NPC Patrol Patterns)

Each NPC has a repeating exercise cycle that changes where they look:

| Exercise | Look Direction | Duration | Safe Window |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------|
| Treadmill | Forward (into mirror) | Constant | Behind them only |
| Squat | Forward, then down at bottom | 4 sec cycle | 1 sec at bottom of squat |
| Deadlift | Down | 3 sec cycle | While lifting (looking down) |
| Overhead press | Up | 3 sec cycle | At top of press (eyes on ceiling) |
| Lateral raise | Sweeps sideways | 3 sec cycle | Almost no safe window |
| Yoga (downward dog) | Between legs (behind them) | 5 sec hold | From the front only |
| Yoga (warrior pose) | Sideways, fixed | 5 sec hold | From the opposite side |
| Plank | Down at floor | 10 sec hold | Generous window, but they're low and easy to trip over |
| Stretching | Variable, unpredictable | Changes | The hard zone. No reliable pattern |

The player learns the patterns. Timing movement to exercise cycles is the core skill. Exactly like learning guard patrol routes in MGS.

## The Comedy (what makes it funny while you play)

**The gym playlist.** Music playing over the speakers. Empowerment anthems. "Independent Women" by Destiny's Child. "Run the World" by Beyonce. The soundtrack is telling you about female power while you're crawling between yoga mats trying not to look at anyone.

**The motivational posters on the walls:**
- "STRONG IS THE NEW BEAUTIFUL" (you glance at the poster, your gaze passes over someone doing squats, the circle starts filling, you snap away)
- "YOUR BODY IS YOUR TEMPLE" (directly above the stretching zone, so looking at the poster means looking at the stretching zone)
- "EMPOWER YOURSELF" (next to the exit, your destination, giving you hope)
- "MEN'S HOUR: TUESDAY 5AM-6AM" (on a small sign by the door, telling you when men are actually welcome to use the gym without fear)

**The gym sounds.** Weights clanking. Treadmill whirring. And occasionally: a phone camera shutter sound. Someone just photographed themselves for Instagram. In a gym where looking at people is a crime, everyone is photographing themselves for public consumption. The contradiction is ambient.

**The water fountain.** Midway through the gym. A potential rest point. The player can stop and drink. Safe: looking at the fountain. But two women are also at the fountain, chatting. They're standing right next to it. Getting a drink without looking at them requires precision. The player carefully positions themselves, looks DOWN at the fountain, drinks, and a woman says: "Excuse me, are you alright? You seem very... focused on that fountain." Being TOO obviously not-looking is its own kind of suspicious.

**The dropped weight.** At one point, an NPC drops a dumbbell. CLANG. Every NPC looks toward the sound. The player's instinct: look too. If they look: the NPC who dropped it is bending over to pick it up. The 2-second timer starts INSTANTLY because the player is now looking at someone bending over. The game is exploiting the player's own reflexes. The sound was a trap.

## The Boss: The Personal Trainer

**She patrols the corridor to the lockers.** Slow walk. Wide vision cone. She's not exercising, she's watching. Her job is to make sure clients feel safe. She does this by looking for men who are looking.

**Her pattern:** Walk to one end of the corridor. Pause for 3 seconds (checks her clipboard). Turn. Walk to the other end. Pause. Turn. Classic MGS guard patrol.

**The twist:** She occasionally stops mid-patrol and turns around without warning. A random direction check. The player must react instantly: phone up, eyes down, or find cover behind a pillar.

**If she catches you:** She doesn't report you through the app. She approaches. "Hi! Is everything okay? You seem a bit... lost. Can I help you find something?" Her tone is pleasant. Her eyes are not. She walks you back toward the entrance. Not ejected. Redirected. You have to start the corridor section again.

**Beating her:** Two options. Wait for her clipboard pause and sprint past (risky, running in the corridor draws attention). Or: another gym member distracts her with a question. This happens on a random timer. When someone asks her a question, she turns away for 8 seconds. That's the window.

---

## The Locker

The player reaches the lockers. Locker 47. It's in the women's changing room corridor.

The final challenge: the locker is visible from the changing room door, which is open. Women are going in and out. The player has 10 seconds between each person entering/leaving. Open the locker (combination: obtained from the Network contact beforehand). Grab the USB. Close the locker. Get out.

If someone exits the changing room while the player is at the locker: "WHAT ARE YOU DOING?" Instant mission fail if the player can't explain themselves. One dialogue option saves it: "Sorry, wrong locker. I'm looking for the men's." The NPC: "Men's lockers are by the entrance." The player: "Thanks." They walk away. They have to come back and try again.

---

## The Exit

The player has the USB. Now cross the entire gym floor AGAIN to reach the door. But the NPCs have rotated exercises. The patterns are different from the way in. The player can't rely on memorised routes.

**The final comedy beat:** The player reaches the door. A woman at the reception desk: "Thanks for visiting EMPOWER! How was your workout?"

Dialogue options:
1. "Great, thanks." (Safe. +0.)
2. "I didn't actually work out." (She looks confused. "Oh. Well. We charge for the full session either way.")
3. Say nothing. Walk out. Eyes on the floor. The most honest response.
