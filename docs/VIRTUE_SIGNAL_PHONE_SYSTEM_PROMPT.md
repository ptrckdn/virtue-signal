## THE PHONE

The player's phone is the game's ambient world-building system. It replaces GTA's radio stations. It's 2026, so the world comments on itself through social media, messaging apps, and news, not through radio DJs.

The phone pulls up with a button press. Time does NOT pause. You can get caught scrolling Virtue during a police chase.

There are four apps on the phone:

---

### APP 1: "Virtue" (X / Twitter clone)

The social media feed. This is where the world talks about itself, about you, and about what's happening in Landanstan.

**How it reacts to the player's Civility Index:**

- GREEN: Normal feed. Memes, outrage, celebrity drama, faction leaders subtweeting each other. Background noise of a dysfunctional society.
- AMBER: Posts start appearing about "someone flagged in your area." Virtue's algorithm starts showing you government safety messaging. Your follower count drops because the algorithm is suppressing your reach.
- RED: Your face appears on Virtue in a "HAVE YOU SEEN THIS PERSON?" post shared by the Metropolitan Compliance Bureau. NPCs who follow you are unfollowing in real time. Piers Pressmore does a live thread about you.
- BLACK: Your Virtue account is suspended. The app still opens but you can only see government messaging and a banner that says "This account has been restricted pending a Community Values Review." You can see other people posting about you but can't respond.

**The feed should include:**

- Piers Pressmore posting every 30 seconds. Outraged about everything. Contradicts himself between posts. His pinned post is a link to his "Uncensored" show which is sponsored by a government-approved media fund.
- Donald Trunk reposting Network content with comments like "These people are TREMENDOUS patriots! The Landanstan government is FAILING!" Every endorsement costs The Network support. The Network begs him to stop. He doesn't.
- Nigel Barrage posting pub selfies with captions about "the real people of this great nation" while geotagged at a members-only club in Mayfair.
- Prince Edmund and Duchess Clarity posting documentary clips about "our truth" from inside a mansion. Comments disabled.
- Zane Polansky posting about climate justice with a Virtue verified checkmark that he got through the old HypnoBoost corporate account.
- Kara Badham announcing another "independent review" that will "report back in due course."
- Random NPCs posting increasingly paranoid things: "Does anyone else feel like their phone is listening?" followed by a government ad for the Community Values Workshop.
- Memes about the Civility Index. "POV: you sneezed too aggressively near a CCTV camera." "My Civility Index after making eye contact with a police officer." "Tag someone whose Civility Index is definitely AMBER."
- Faction recruitment posts disguised as lifestyle content. The Composters post infographics. The Patriots post outrage clips. The Liberators post theory quotes over sunset photos. The Old Guard don't post at all because they communicate through private dinners.
- Promoted posts from the government: "Your Civility Index is YOUR responsibility. Download the Community Values app today." The promoted posts increase in frequency as your meter rises.
- After Mission 5 (the broadcast): the entire feed is about what you did. Split reactions. Some people call you a hero. Some call you a terrorist. The government posts a statement. Pressmore does a 47-post thread. Trunk reposts the broadcast clip. It has 2.3 million views.

**WRITERS' ROOM TASK:** Write 50-80 Virtue posts covering all meter levels. Include posts from every named character. Include 20+ memes. Include 10+ government promoted posts. Include the post-Mission 5 reaction thread. These should be funny individually but devastating collectively. The feed should feel like scrolling real Twitter in 2026 if the worst predictions came true.

### APP 2: "WhatsThat" (WhatsApp clone)

The early-game messaging app. Mission briefings, faction contacts, and casual NPC conversations come through here.

**Key features:**

- Group chats for each faction the player is involved with
- Mission givers send voice notes (text displayed, can add voice later)
- The "read receipts" are blue ticks that the government can see. NPCs occasionally say "don't put that on WhatsThat" or "call me, not text"
- The government sends you automated messages: "Your Community Values Workshop is overdue. Tap here to schedule." These increase in frequency as your meter rises.
- At AMBER+, some contacts stop replying. Their last message is "Sorry, can't talk right now." They're being watched too.
- Faction leaders have distinct texting styles. Barrage sends voice notes that ramble for 3 minutes. Harmer sends perfectly punctuated messages that read like official correspondence. Zane sends links to petitions. The Network contact sends self-deleting messages.
- There's a family group chat that has nothing to do with the game. Mum sends photos of the dog. Dad asks how to use the new TV remote. Sister shares a recipe. This exists purely to make the phone feel real and to make the surveillance state feel more invasive when you realise the government can read this too.

**WRITERS' ROOM TASK:** Write the message threads for each faction contact's first interaction with the player. Write the family group chat (10-15 messages of completely mundane family content). Write the government automated messages at each meter level (GREEN through BLACK). Write the moment where contacts go silent at AMBER.

### APP 3: "Crypt" (Telegram clone)

The encrypted messaging app. The Network operates exclusively on Crypt.

**The download moment:**

This is a major story beat. A Network contact tells you "We need to move to Crypt. WhatsThat isn't safe." The player opens the app store (in-game). The Crypt app page has a government warning banner: "This application has been flagged by the Digital Safety Commission. Downloading it will be noted on your Digital Citizenship Record."

The player chooses to download it. Their Civility Index immediately jumps +10. Just for having the app on their phone.

This is the point of no return. The game explicitly tells you the consequences and you choose to do it anyway. The moment is designed to make the player feel what it's like to self-censor and then choose not to. That hesitation, that split second where you think "should I?", is the game's entire thesis statement in one interaction.

**After downloading:**

- All Network communications move to Crypt
- Messages auto-delete after being read
- The Network contact's profile picture is a crusader cross icon (the forbidden iconography)
- Mission briefings are more detailed here because the characters feel safe
- BUT: at BLACK level, the government cracks Crypt. Messages start appearing with "[DECRYPTED BY DIGITAL SAFETY COMMISSION]" headers. The safe space was never safe. The encryption was compromised from the start. The Network didn't know.

**WRITERS' ROOM TASK:** Write the "should I download Crypt?" dialogue sequence. Write the first Network message after you've downloaded it (the relief of being "safe"). Write the moment at BLACK level when the player realises the encryption was compromised. Write 5-10 Network mission briefings that feel more honest and vulnerable than the WhatsThat versions because the characters think they're not being watched.

### APP 4: "LandanNews" (BBC News clone)

The news app. Headlines reflect the game state in real time.

**How it evolves:**

- Early game: Bland, reassuring. "Minister praises 'record compliance levels' across Landanstan." "Community Values Workshops see 15% increase in attendance." "Weather: Overcast with a chance of monitoring."
- Mid game: Cracks showing. "Opposition questions effectiveness of Civility Index after protest incident." "Report suggests 'minor inconsistencies' in police stop-and-search data." "Comment: Why the Civility Index is actually good for civil liberties (sponsored content)."
- Late game: Barely holding together. "Government dismisses leaked documents as 'manipulated content.'" "Prominent broadcaster questions: 'Have we gone too far?'" "Emergency powers extended for sixth consecutive month."
- After Mission 5: "Illegal broadcast reaches estimated 2.3 million devices." "Government announces 'Operation Digital Shield' in response to signal breach." "PM Harmer: 'This was an attack on our shared values.'" "Pressmore: 'I've been saying this for years.'"
- Endgame: "Former resistance figure [PLAYER NAME] flagged under Historical Statements Review." The news reports on YOUR downfall using the exact same language it used to report on others earlier in the game.

**The satire of the news itself:**

- Every article has a "Funded by the Digital Safety Commission" tag at the bottom
- Comments are "disabled for community safety"
- The "Most Read" sidebar always has one genuinely important story buried under four celebrity stories
- Occasionally a real piece of journalism appears and then disappears from the app, replaced by "This article has been removed pending review"
- The weather section includes an "Outdoor Gathering Risk Level" alongside the actual weather
- There's a "Good News" section that's exclusively stories about compliance achievements and workshop attendance records

**WRITERS' ROOM TASK:** Write 30-40 headlines across all game stages (early, mid, late, post-Mission 5, endgame). Write 5 full "articles" (3-4 paragraphs each) that the player can tap into for key story moments. Write the "Most Read" sidebar lists. Write the disappeared article moment. Write the endgame headline about the player's downfall. Every headline should be funny on its own but the progression from bland reassurance to barely concealed authoritarianism should be genuinely unsettling.

### APP 5: "Your Digital Citizenship Report" (pre-installed, cannot be deleted)

The government's own app for tracking your Civility Index.

- Shows your current score with a smiley face (GREEN), neutral face (AMBER), frowning face (RED), or skull icon (BLACK)
- Lists every infraction: "14:32 - CCTV detection in restricted zone. +2." "14:35 - Association with flagged individual. +3."
- Has a "Tips to improve your score" section with suggestions like "Consider attending a Community Values Workshop" and "Avoid prolonged contact with individuals whose Civility Index exceeds 25"
- Has a "Report a concern" button where you can snitch on NPCs for a meter reduction
- The "Terms and Conditions" link goes to a 400-page document that nobody reads but which includes the line "By continuing to exist within Landanstan, you agree to ongoing monitoring"
- At BLACK level, the app displays: "Your Digital Citizenship has been suspended. Please report to your nearest Compliance Centre."
- The app sends push notifications that appear during gameplay: "Reminder: your Civility Index has risen above the community average. This has been noted."

**WRITERS' ROOM TASK:** Write 15-20 infractions log entries covering different offences. Write the "Tips to improve your score" for each meter level (they get more desperate and threatening as the meter rises). Write the Terms and Conditions highlights (the absurd buried clauses). Write 10 push notification texts. Write the "Report a concern" flow where the player snitches on an NPC.

### THE PHONE AS A WHOLE

The phone should feel like a real phone. The player should find themselves doomscrolling Virtue when they should be driving. The family group chat should make them feel guilty about the life they're risking. The Crypt download should make them hesitate. The Digital Citizenship Report should make them feel watched.

The phone is not a menu. It's a second game running alongside the first one. The city is the physical world. The phone is the digital world. Both are surveilled. Both react to the player's choices. Both are funny and both are terrifying.

The moment a player checks their phone after a police chase and sees their own face on Virtue with "HAVE YOU SEEN THIS PERSON?" trending... that's a clip. That's the viral moment. That's the screenshot that gets shared with the caption "this game is insane."

Give me everything. Posts, messages, headlines, notifications, memes, articles. Fill the phone with a living, breathing digital Landanstan that's just as detailed as the physical one.
