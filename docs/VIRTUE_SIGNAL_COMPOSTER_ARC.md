# VIRTUE SIGNAL: The Composter Arc

## The Merge

The Composters absorb the Liberators. One faction, two wings, constant internal friction. The greens (Shoreditch, warehouse conversions, Zane Polansky) and the radicals (Brixton, trust fund houses, direct action). They agree on the diagnosis: the system is oppressive, the planet is dying, the poor are exploited. They cannot agree on anything else.

Territory: Shoreditch (green wing HQ) + Brixton (radical wing HQ). Two bases, one faction, permanent argument about which base hosts the meetings.

This brings the faction count from 7 to 6: Centrists, Patriots, Composters, Old Guard, Restorationists, The Network.

---

## The Fixed Idea

"The system is broken. We know how to fix it. If only people would listen."

Every factual claim the Composters make is correct. The surveillance state IS oppressive. The housing crisis IS manufactured. Climate change IS real. The asylum system IS a money laundering operation for Old Guard property developers. The Composters are the smartest faction in the room.

They are also completely useless.

---

## The Character Arc (Dostoyevsky)

The player starts with a fixed idea: "These people understand the problem. They have the answer."

The game puts PEOPLE in front of that idea. Not arguments. Faces.

The player joins the Composters because they're right. Across 10 missions, the player discovers that being right is a kind of trap. The Composters are so certain of their analysis that the certainty itself becomes the product. They don't need to act because knowing is enough. The movement exists to make its members feel like they're on the right side. The feeling IS the output.

The fixed idea cracks not because the Composters are wrong, but because being right changes nothing. The distance between knowing the answer and living it is the entire arc.

---

## The Greene Layer

The most meaningful virtue in this arc comes from compromised people.

Zane, the former fraud, does more practical good than Seren, the ideological purist. Dele, who grew up in Restorationist territory and still talks like it, feeds more people than Miriam's book has readers. Rio, who owes the movement everything and is being eaten by it, is the only Composter who actually builds something that lasts.

The player arrives at the Composters with clean hands. That's the problem. The Composters are a faction for people with clean hands. By the end, the player's hands are dirty, and that's when they're finally useful.

## Key Characters

**Zane Polansky** (faction leader, green wing)
Former wellness entrepreneur. His old brand "HypnoBoost" sold boob enlargement hypnosis tapes. Faded billboards throughout Shoreditch. He's reinvented himself as a climate activist and community organiser. Genuinely effective at organising. Genuinely believes now. Cannot escape his past. Every serious moment is undercut by someone finding another HypnoBoost billboard.

He's not a fraud. That's what makes him complicated. He WAS a fraud. He changed. But the internet never forgets, and neither does the game. Both things are Zane.

On the movement: "We had a planning meeting for the planning meeting about the planning meeting. Someone brought hummus. It was really good hummus."

**Seren Oakes** (radical wing leader)
28. Trust fund. Father is a City lawyer. Mother sits on the board of a private school. Seren lives in Brixton in a house her parents bought for cash. Genuinely brilliant political theorist. Can quote Gramsci, Fanon, hooks, and Chomsky from memory. Has never had a job.

She is not a hypocrite. She KNOWS she's a contradiction. She hates the wealth that funds her activism. She can't give it up because without it she'd be powerless. The guilt fuels the radicalism. The radicalism justifies the guilt. It's a closed loop.

On the house: "Yes, my parents bought it. Yes, it's worth 1.2 million. Yes, I run a housing justice campaign from the kitchen. If you can see the contradiction, congratulations. So can I. Now what?"

**Dele Okonkwo** (ground-level organiser)
34. Nigerian-British. Grew up on a council estate in Elephant & Castle (Restorationist territory). Actually working class. Joined the Composters because they were the only faction that helped his mum when the council tried to demolish her block. Runs the food bank. Does the real work.

Dele is the Margaret of this arc. The working-class person the movement claims to represent, who the movement actually fails. The Composters talk about the working class. Dele IS the working class. The gap between their theory about him and his reality is where the deepest comedy and deepest pain live.

On meetings: "They spent two hours debating whether the food bank should use the word 'bank' because of its capitalist connotations. Meanwhile the food went cold."

**Professor Miriam Hartley** (the intellectual)
58. Cambridge. Wrote the book on digital surveillance that half the Composters haven't read but all of them cite. Advises the faction on strategy. Privately thinks they're all children. The only Composter who's actually dangerous to the system because she understands how it works at a structural level. Helen Cross (Centrist) considers her the only real threat.

On the Composters: "They're not a movement. They're a support group for people who feel bad about feeling good."

**Rio** (the one who was helped)
22. Non-binary. Homeless at 17. The Composters' outreach programme got them off the street, into a shared house, into education. Rio is the movement's proof of concept: actual, measurable, real help given to a real person. The Composters use Rio's story at every fundraiser, every public event, every media appearance.

Rio is the Yusuf of this arc. Genuinely helped. Now being consumed. Not maliciously. Just inevitably. Movements need stories. Stories need people. People get used up.

On being a success story: "They put me on the poster. Literally. I'm on the poster outside the community centre. It says 'LIVES CHANGED.' I'm the life. I didn't get asked."

## The Missions

### L1: The Assembly (Social)
**Topic:** Performative allyship
**Setup:** Composter general assembly in the Shoreditch warehouse. 40 people. Agenda has 12 items. Two hours in, they're on item 3. Someone challenges the agenda's font as "exclusionary." Zane tries to steer. Seren interrupts with a point of process.

**Gameplay:** Dialogue navigation. The player must get ONE practical proposal passed before the meeting collapses. Every option spawns a counter-proposal, an amendment, and a protest. The path through requires Dele (the only one who'll second a motion) and a procedural trick (calling the question) that forces a vote.

**The joke:** The proposal that passes is comically minor. "Agreed: the food bank will open on Thursdays as well as Tuesdays." Three hours of democracy for Thursday sandwiches.

**Meter:** +3

### L2: The Mural (Physical/Chase)
**Topic:** Free speech / art
**Setup:** Painting a mural in Shoreditch. The Civility Index as a snake eating its own tail. Council enforcement arrives. Protect the painters long enough to finish.

**Gameplay:** Timed lookout and diversion. Chase enforcement through back streets. Return. Painters need 3 more minutes. Another team from the other direction. Divert again. Mural complete.

**The twist:** Beautiful. Powerful. Viral on Virtue. 50,000 shares. The council removes it overnight. Zane: "a victory for awareness." Dele: "a wall with new paint on it." Both are right.

**Meter:** +5

### L3: The Food Bank (Social/Puzzle)
**Topic:** Cost of living / class
**Setup:** Dele runs the food bank. Donations down. Faction treasury has funds. Seren wants them for "direct action infrastructure." Zane wants a "community resilience event." Dele wants food.

**Gameplay:** Budget puzzle. Three competing demands, limited funds. Every option has a real cost. If the food bank closes, people don't eat.

**The deeper layer:** The player processes food bank clients. One is a family from the asylum hotel in Elephant & Castle. Tommy Lemon's territory. The people Tommy wants gone. They're hungry. They don't care about your faction. The food bank doesn't check politics.

**Meter:** +2

### L4: The Podcast (Social/Chase)
**Topic:** Media / cancel culture
**Setup:** Miriam invited on Max Contrarian's podcast. Seren calls it platforming the enemy. The vote is 19-18 in favour. Seren walks out.

**Gameplay:** Phase 1: Drive Miriam to the studio. Avoid Composter hardliners blockading the building. Phase 2: Feed Miriam talking points via WhatsThat during the interview. Wrong points and she's trapped. Right points and she's devastating.

**The result:** Miriam is brilliant. Clip goes viral. And the Composters tear themselves apart. Faction chat is a civil war. Miriam accused of "legitimising the discourse." Seren starts a splinter meeting.

**Meter:** +4

### L5: The Garden (Stealth/Physical)
**Topic:** Housing crisis
**Setup:** Old Guard property developer sitting on a derelict Brixton site. The Composters plan overnight guerrilla occupation. Community garden by dawn.

**Gameplay:** Night stealth. Avoid cameras and a watchman. Carry in soil and raised beds. Watchman wakes. Distract. By dawn the garden exists. Seren chains herself to the fence for the cameras.

**What happens next:** The garden is beautiful. Community uses it. Three weeks later, bulldozed by injunction. But during those three weeks, Rio starts spending time there. Quietly. Not for the cameras. Just sitting in the sun. That's real.

**Meter:** +8

### L6: The Conference (Puzzle/Social)
**Topic:** Performative allyship / institutional capture
**Setup:** Annual Composter conference. Workshops on "Decolonising Resistance." The player attends as Zane's assistant.

**Gameplay:** Navigate the schedule. In one workshop, discover that Civic Futures Foundation (the Centrist think tank funding Barrage) has ALSO been funding Composter events. Laundered through a "grassroots arts grant."

**The mirror:** The Barrage arc reveals he's controlled opposition. This mission reveals the Composters might be too. Not through direct control. Through funding that makes them comfortable enough to never actually threaten the system.

**Meter:** +3

### L7: The Eviction (Chase/Choice)
**Topic:** Housing crisis / class
**Setup:** Dele's mum's council block in Elephant & Castle is being emptied for demolition. Last holdout. The Composters promise support. Seren organises a "solidarity camp." Tents, banners, megaphones. Media arrives.

**Gameplay:** Bailiffs arrive with police escort. The choice: stay with the camp (symbolic, safe, on camera) or go inside the building with Dele's mum (dangerous, effective, off camera).

If the player stays outside, Seren gets her media moment. Dele's mum is removed. Block demolished.

If the player goes inside, they buy 48 hours. Enough for Miriam's emergency injunction. It works. Nobody films it. Nobody tweets. Seren is furious because the narrative was "ruined."

This is the O'Connor moment. Grace in a corridor. No audience. The ideology of solidarity meeting the physical reality of standing in a doorway.

**Dele (if player went inside):** "You showed up. Nobody else did. The people with the megaphones were outside. You were in the corridor."

**Dele (if player stayed outside):** Nothing. He stops answering messages for two days.

**Meter:** +6 to +12

### L8: The Split (Social/Stealth)
**Topic:** Internal politics / redemption
**Setup:** Seren calls a no-confidence vote against Zane. HypnoBoost is the weapon. Screenshots, archives, customer complaints. "Our leader sold fake breast enlargement to vulnerable women."

**Gameplay:** Two simultaneous objectives. In the meeting: gather votes for Zane or side with Seren. On the phone: Miriam has found that Seren's parents donated to the Conservative Party. Leak it or hold it.

Is Zane's past relevant? He changed. But he profited from exploitation. The game doesn't answer. The player votes.

**If Zane survives:** Faction holds, weakened. Seren stays, bitter.
**If Seren wins:** Zane expelled. Faction radicalises. Dele stops attending.
**If player leaks the donation:** Seren destroyed. But the player used surveillance tactics. The Centrists' tactics. Miriam notices. She says nothing. She doesn't have to.

**Meter:** +4

### L9: The March (Combat/Survival)
**Topic:** Free speech / state power
**Setup:** March against the Civility Index. Miriam's speech. Dele's logistics. Seren's banners. 5,000 people. The biggest protest Landanstan has seen.

**Gameplay:** Peaceful until Westminster. Police kettle the march. The player leads a breakout. The crowd fragments. Choose who to stay with:

**Zane:** Media scrum. Changes nothing.
**Seren:** Police confrontation. Riot. Discredits the movement.
**Dele:** Residential streets. Nothing visible. Saves three people from being trampled.
**Miriam:** Camera position. Documents police overreach. Changes the conversation.

If the player follows Dele, the world goes quiet for three seconds. The Shakespeare consideration. Crowd noise drops away. Just footsteps on a side street. No prompt. No reward. Stillness.

**Meter:** BLACK (temporary)

### L10: The Garden Again (Walking)
**Topic:** What remains
**Setup:** Three months after the march. The garden in Brixton has been rebuilt. Not by the Composters. By the residents. Without banners.

**Three locations:**

**The garden.** Rio is there. Watering plants. They quit the Composters. Studying horticulture. "I stopped being a poster. I started being a gardener. Same dirt, better outcome."

**Dele's mum's flat** (if saved). She makes tea. "Those friends of yours. The ones with the banners. My boy says they mean well. I say meaning well and a pound coin will get you a cup of tea."

**The warehouse.** Composter HQ. A planning meeting for the next protest. Or a conference about the last one. Or a workshop about the one before that.

The player leaves the warehouse. Walks past the garden. Rio is there. Two kids from the estate are helping plant tomatoes. Nobody is filming. Nobody is tweeting. It's just a garden.

## The Composter Ending

The ending gives the player what they asked for.

**The victory:** Coalition government with Composter influence. Green policies enacted. Surveillance state reformed. Civility Index renamed "Community Harmony Score." Function identical. Now also monitors "environmental social responsibility."

- Meat restricted to two servings per week, enforced by purchase tracking
- Private car ownership requires carbon offset the working class can't afford
- Green zones have clean air, parks, bike lanes
- Council estates have rolling blackouts
- Zane on television: the blackouts are "a transition cost"
- Dele has left Landanstan. Final message: "They built a new system. Same shape. Green paint."
- Rio's garden requisitioned for a "community energy co-operative." Solar panels where the tomatoes were.

**The mirror.** White text on black screen. Every quiet choice.

**Jimmy's number.**

**The bell.** Then silence.

---

## The Structure

The Restorationist arc: prejudice meets a face. Margaret and Yusuf. The fixed idea is "my people are being abandoned." It cracks when the player meets someone who is both the problem and not the problem.

The Composter arc: idealism meets its own reflection. Dele and Rio. The fixed idea is "we know how to fix it." It cracks when the player discovers that being right isn't enough. The movement fails its own people. Not through malice. Through the comfortable distance between knowing and doing.

Both things are Dele: grateful for the help, invisible to the helpers.

Both things are Rio: saved by the movement, consumed by the movement.

The garden is the chapel. The one place nobody is performing. Father Brennan opens the door. Rio waters the plants. Neither asks for an audience.
